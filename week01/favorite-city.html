Facts About South Korea From The Web.

South Korea (Republic of Korea) is a highly developed East Asian nation with a population of over 51 million, known for its rapid modernization, top-tier technology, and vibrant culture. Roughly 70% of its land is mountainous, with a capital, Seoul, that holds half the population. It is famous for its "Pali-pali" (hurry, hurry) culture, tech innovation, and K-pop. South Korea is celebrated for its highly competitive education system and dynamic, innovation-driven job market, though both feature intense societal expectations. While the country offers robust global opportunities, individuals pursuing these paths must navigate a high-pressure academic culture. The South Korean government actively promotes higher education to foreigners. Platforms like Study in Korea are excellent resources to explore universities and government-backed scholarships designed to attract international talent. Multinational firms and tech startups in cities like Seoul frequently seek foreigners for roles in IT, software development, engineering, and digital marketing. South Korean cuisine is deeply rooted in balance, fermentation, and sharing, offering flavors that range from fiery and savory to comforting and mild. Meals are historically centered around rice, soup, and an array of side dishes called banchan.
